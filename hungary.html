University of Carthage Hungarian Scholarships 2026

Fully Funded Opportunities for Bachelor, Master & PhD under Stipendium Hungaricum Program

Deadline: 15 January 2026

Overview

The University of Carthage Hungarian Scholarships 2026 offers an exceptional, fully funded pathway for students to study in Hungary. Through the prestigious Stipendium Hungaricum program—a collaboration between the Tunisian Ministry of Higher Education and the Hungarian Ministry of Foreign Affairs—250 scholarships are available for the 2026–2027 academic year. Selected students will have the opportunity to pursue Bachelor’s, Master’s, or PhD degrees at top Hungarian public universities across fields like Engineering, IT, Health Sciences, and more.

Country

Hungary

Study Level

Bachelor, Masters, PhD

Program

Stipendium Hungaricum

Scholarship Benefits

Successful applicants will receive comprehensive financial support for the duration of their studies:

  • Full Tuition Fee Waiver: Complete exemption from tuition costs.
  • Monthly Stipend (Bachelor/Master): 43,700 HUF per month.
  • Monthly Stipend (PhD): 140,000 HUF (1st phase) to 180,000 HUF (2nd phase).
  • Accommodation Support: Free dormitory place or 40,000 HUF/month housing allowance.
  • Health Insurance: Comprehensive medical insurance for the entire duration.

Available Study Fields

Applicants can choose from a wide variety of disciplines, including but not limited to:

Engineering Information Technology Natural Sciences Health Sciences Agriculture Economics Architecture

Eligibility Criteria

  • Nationality: Primarily for Tunisian citizens (nominated by University of Carthage), though Stipendium Hungaricum is open to many partner countries.
  • Academic Timeline: Candidates must be able to complete necessary coursework by August 1st, 2026.
  • Language Proficiency: Must meet the language requirements of the chosen program (English, French, or Hungarian). A preparatory year is available if needed.
  • Restrictions: Previous Stipendium Hungaricum winners cannot reapply for the same study level.
  • PhD Candidates: Must submit a research proposal and potentially secure supervisor approval.

Required Documents

  • Updated CV / Resume
  • Degree Certificates & Academic Transcripts
  • Motivation Letter / Statement of Purpose
  • Recommendation Letters
  • Proof of Language Proficiency
  • Research Proposal (for Graduate students)
