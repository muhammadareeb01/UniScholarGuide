Eligibility Criteria

To be eligible for the CSC Scholarship, you must meet the following general requirements:

  • Nationality: Must be a non-Chinese citizen in good health.
  • Education & Age:
    • BS Applicants: High school diploma, under 25 years old.
    • MS Applicants: Bachelor’s degree, under 35 years old.
    • PhD Applicants: Master’s degree, under 40 years old.
  • Academic Record: Good academic performance and research ability.
  • Language: English proficiency (IELTS/TOEFL or English Proficiency Certificate) or HSK for Chinese taught programs.
  • No Dual Scholarship: Should not be holding any other scholarship in China.

Required Documents

  • CSC Application Form (Generate from Portal)
  • Notarized Highest Degree Certificate
  • Academic Transcripts (Notarized)
  • Study Plan / Research Proposal (800+ words)
  • Two Recommendation Letters (Professors/Assoc. Profs)
  • Passport Copy (Valid for >1 year)
  • Physical Examination Form (Foreigner Physical Exam)
  • Police Clearance Certificate (Non-criminal record)
  • English Proficiency Certificate (IELTS not strictly mandatory)
  • Acceptance Letter (Optional but increases chances)
Note: Ensure all documents are scanned clearly. Some universities may require hard copies mailed to them.

Step-by-Step Application Process

Step 1: Find a University & Agency Number

Search for Chinese universities offering CSC scholarships in your field. Note down their unique Agency Number (e.g., Zhejiang University: 10335). You will need this for the CSC portal.

Step 2: Register on CSC Portal

Create an account on Campuschina.org. Fill in your profile details.

Step 3: Select Category & Agency

Select Program Category Type B (for University route). Enter the Agency Number of your chosen university. The university name should appear automatically.

Step 4: Upload Documents

Upload all required documents (Diplomas, Transcripts, Physical Exam, etc.) in PDF format. Ensure they are legible.

Step 5: Apply to University Portal

Visit the university's own admission website. Create an account and submit the admission application there as well. Sometimes, an application fee is required (approx 400-800 RMB).

Step 6: Wait for Results

Universities review applications from March to May. Final CSC results are usually announced in July - August.
