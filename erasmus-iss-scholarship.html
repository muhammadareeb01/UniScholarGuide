Scholarship Summary

Erasmus University Rotterdam ISS Scholarship 2026 is designed for international students aiming to pursue a master’s degree at the International Institute of Social Studies (ISS) in the Netherlands. This prestigious scholarship supports outstanding students who demonstrate academic excellence and leadership potential, helping them cover tuition fees and advance their careers in development studies.

At ISS, most students study with the help of partial scholarships, and the university provides a comprehensive database of funding opportunities based on your country of origin. By selecting your country, you can explore a tailored list of scholarships to support your studies. The ISS Scholarship Fund specifically targets talented students who do not have other funding sources, ensuring that deserving candidates can access world-class education at Erasmus University Rotterdam.

Scholarship Benefits

The ISS Scholarship Fund for Excellent Students covers 40% of the tuition fee. Note that it is a partially funded opportunity.

Eligibility Criteria

To be eligible for the ISS Scholarship Fund for Excellent Students, candidates must fulfill the following requirements:

  • Required Language: English
  • Eligible Countries: All countries
  • You wish to enroll in the Master of Arts in Development Studies at ISS.
  • You have a letter of admission from ISS.
  • You have relevant work experience, a degree in one of the social sciences (upper second or first class), and a strong motivation to study at ISS, OR
  • You are a recent graduate with a first-class degree in one of the social sciences and a strong motivation to study at ISS.

How to Apply?

  1. Make sure you meet the eligibility criteria for the MA in Development Studies at ISS, including relevant academic background and professional experience.
  2. Secure your admission letter from ISS before applying for the scholarship, as only admitted students can apply.
  3. Prepare a one-page motivation statement explaining why you deserve the scholarship, highlighting your achievements, career goals, and interest in ISS.
  4. Provide detailed financial documents, such as bank statements, to show you can cover tuition, living expenses, and health insurance not included in the scholarship.
  5. Submit your motivation statement and financial documents via email to admission@iss.nl before the deadline of 15 June 2026.
  6. Wait for confirmation; selected applicants will receive notification and instructions for the next steps from the ISS student office.

Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

Who is eligible for the Erasmus University Rotterdam ISS Scholarship 2026?

Eligibility is open to all nationalities wishing to enroll in the MA in Development Studies at ISS. Candidates must have a strong academic record, relevant work experience, or a first-class social sciences degree with clear motivation to study at ISS.

What does the scholarship cover?

The scholarship covers 40% of the tuition fee for the master’s program. It does not include living expenses, health insurance, or travel costs.

Can recent graduates apply?

Yes, recent graduates with a first-class degree in social sciences and a strong motivation to study at ISS are eligible to apply.

How do I apply?

Applicants must email a one-page motivation statement and detailed financial information to the ISS admission office (admission@iss.nl).

Is the scholarship fully funded?

No, the scholarship only covers 40% of tuition fees. Students must cover the remaining 60% of tuition, living expenses, and health insurance independently.

Do I need an admission letter before applying?

Yes, you must have an official admission letter from ISS before applying for the scholarship.
