Hardiman PhD Scholarships 2026

Fully Funded Research Opportunity at University of Galway, Ireland

Deadline: 6 February 2026

Overview

The Hardiman PhD Scholarships 2026 offer a premier fully funded opportunity for aspiring researchers from around the globe to study at the University of Galway in Ireland. This prestigious program invites talented individuals to engage in creative and innovative research across four key pillars: Innovation for Health; Creativity, Culture & Society; Sustainable & Resilient Environments; and Transformative Data & AI. Selected scholars will join a vibrant academic community and receive comprehensive financial support to pursue their Structured PhD.

Country

Ireland

Scholarship Benefits

The Hardiman Scholarship provides exceptional financial support:

  • Full Tuition Fees: Covered for the entire 4-year duration.
  • Generous Stipend: An annual tax-free stipend of €25,000.
  • Research Opportunity: Access to world-class facilities and diverse research programs.

Research Areas

Scholarships are offered in four unique thematic pillars:

1. Innovation for Health: Innovative healthcare policies and practices.
2. Creativity, Culture & Society: Global cultural practices across time.
3. Sustainable & Resilient Environments: Marine, environment, planetary health, and decarbonization.
4. Transformative Data & AI: Data semantics, analytics, AI, and machine learning.

Eligibility Criteria

  • Academic Requirement: A first or upper second-class honors degree (or equivalent).
  • Status: Applicants must NOT be currently enrolled as PhD students or have already completed a PhD.
  • Language: Must fulfill the English language proficiency requirements of the University of Galway.
  • Nationality: Open to all international students.

Application Process

Step 1: Find a Supervisor

Identify and contact a PhD supervisor from the University of Galway to discuss your research plan. Find a Supervisor

Step 2: Complete Forms

Fill out the Hardiman Scholarship Application Form with your personal details, CV, and research proposal.

Step 3: Supervisor & Reference Forms

Have your supervisor complete the Supervisor Form and your two referees complete the Academic Reference Forms. These must be emailed to hrscholar@universityofgalway.ie.

Step 4: Submission

Email your completed Application Form (as a PDF with your name in the filename) to hrscholar@universityofgalway.ie with your full name in the subject line.
