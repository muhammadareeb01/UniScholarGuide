Why Study in Italy?

Regional Scholarships

Almost every student gets €7000/year stipend based on family income (DSU, ER.GO, LazioDisco). No high grades required.

Free Accommodation & Meals

Scholarship holders receive free hostel accommodation and daily meal cards (Mensa) for university cafeterias.

Schengen Visa Access

Hold an Italian Resident Permit and travel freely to 27+ European countries (France, Germany, Swiss) during breaks.

Scholarship Opportunities in Italy

Most Popular

Regional Scholarships (DSU, EDISU, LAZIODISCO)

Funded by regional governments, these are the most common scholarships for international students, primarily based on family income (Need-Based).

Value: Up to €7,200 per year
Tuition: 100% Fee Waiver
Benefits: Free Accommodation & Daily Meals
Requirement: Low Family Income (ISEE Parificato)
Government

MAECI Government Scholarship

Offered by the Ministry of Foreign Affairs for Masters and PhD programs to foster international cooperation.

Stipend: €900 Monthly allowance
Insurance: Health insurance covered
Duration: 6 or 9 months (Renewable)
Age Limit: 28 (Masters), 30 (PhD)
Merit Based

University Merit Scholarships

Specific to each university (e.g., Politecnico di Milano, Bologna, Padua). Awarded to top-tier students.

Criteria: High GPA, SAT/GRE scores, CV
Coverage: Variable (Tuition Waiver + Stipend)
Deadline: Usually earlier than general admission

Required Documents Checklist

For University Admission

  • Valid Passport (Min. 1 year validity)
  • Academic Transcripts (10th, 12th, Bachelors Degree)
  • Europass CV (Professional resume format)
  • Motivation Letter / SOP (1-2 pages)
  • Language Certificate (IELTS 6.0+ or Medium of Instruction)
  • Recommendation Letters (2 Academic/Professional)

For Visa (Type D)

  • Universitaly Summary (Pre-enrollment)
  • DOV or CIMEA (Diploma Verification)
  • Proof of Funds (~€6,000 in bank account)
  • Accommodation Proof (Hotel booking or Hostel contract)
  • Health Insurance (Coverage of €30,000)
  • Return Flight Ticket (Dummy ticket acceptable)

For Scholarship (Regional)

  • Family Income Certificate (Preceding tax year)
  • Family Status Certificate (List of family members)
  • Property Certificate (Assets/Property details)
  • Legalized Translations (Italian translation of all above)
Note: These documents must be apostilled/legalized by the Italian Embassy in your country.
