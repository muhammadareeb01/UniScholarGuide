Scholarship Description

The Fulbright Foreign Student Program enables graduate students, young professionals and artists from abroad to study and conduct research in the United States. The Fulbright Foreign Student Program operates in more than 160 countries worldwide. Approximately 4,000 foreign students receive Fulbright scholarships each year.

Scholarship Benefits

  • Full tuition fee waiver for the duration of the study.
  • Round-trip international airfare.
  • Monthly stipend for living expenses.
  • Health insurance (ASPE).
  • Books and material allowance.
  • Visa support and fees.

Eligibility Criteria

  • Must be citizens of a participating country.
  • Must hold a Bachelor's degree (16 years of education) for Masters program.
  • Must hold a Master's degree (18 years of education) for PhD program.
  • Strong academic history.
  • English proficiency (TOEFL/GRE scores may be required).
  • Must not have dual US citizenship.

Required Documents

  • Academic Transcripts and Degrees
  • Statement of Purpose (SOP)
  • Study/Research Objective
  • Three Letters of Recommendation
  • CV / Resume
  • TOEFL / GRE scores (if applicable)

How to Apply (Step-by-Step)

  1. Check the specific requirements for your country on the official Fulbright website.
  2. Prepare your application materials, including essays and references.
  3. Create an account on the online application system.
  4. Complete the forms and upload all required documents.
  5. Submit your application before the deadline.
  6. If shortlisted, you will be called for an interview.

Frequently Asked Questions

Is IELTS mandatory?

It depends on the specific country requirements, but usually, TOEFL is preferred for US scholarships.

Can I apply if I am in my final year?

Yes, usually you can apply if you will complete your degree before the scholarship start date.
